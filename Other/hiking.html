Guide to Hiking

Hiking vs. Backpacking
Hiking:
  • Short Distance, typically a day hike
  • Good for some brief exercise
  • Does not involve Camping, hopefully, if it does its one night
Camping:
  • Long distance, longer than 12 miles usually!
  • Good for getting away long term!
  • Involves staying one + nights always
    Tips
  • Start small, the biggest mistake hikers make is going big before having any previous experience
  • Get a water filter or iodine tablets to purify water on backpacking tricks
  • Dont pack cans of food, keep packaging plastic and minimal
  • Watch your backpack weight, keep it under 15-25lbs.
  • Check weather and dress appropriately

How to Start:
  1. Decide where you want to hike at, if its your first time, I reccomend somewhere you can maintain cell service and that is frequently traveled.
  2. Find someone to go with you, if you can't then make sure multiple people know where you will be and when you should get back.
  3. Plan your trip, how many days should the selected trail take, how many meals, look at trail reviews to know if it is rocky, snowy, well marked, etc.
  4. Once you have the logistics prepare meals, Commond Foods Include:
    • Fruit Cups, Crackers, Peanut Butter, Bagged Chicken pieces, Tortillas, Jelly, and Oatmeal
    • Avoid Cans, Dairy products (unless shelf life is 3+ months out, overly complex meals, and meats that are uncooked.
  5. Make sure you have the proper equipment which is discussed more in the Gear section!
